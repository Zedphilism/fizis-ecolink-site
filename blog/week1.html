Week 1 • Proposal Phase

Brainstorming & Deciding Our HCI Project

by Team FIZIS • October 2025

How It Started

Week 1 was all about ideas. Our FIZIS team kicked off by listing every possible community problem we could think of — from digital health apps to campus navigation tools, waste management, pet adoption, and even a mental-wellness journaling site. The goal was to explore, not to judge. We wanted something practical, people-oriented, and relevant to our surroundings.

Our Brainstorming Session

We started with a mind-map session using Google Jamboard, dumping raw ideas under themes like “environment,” “education,” “campus life,” and “community services.” Then we used a “dot-voting” activity to identify which topics felt most meaningful and achievable within a semester.

  • • Each member pitched at least two ideas with a quick rationale and possible users.
  • • We looked at problems we personally face — like poor waste info in our neighbourhoods, or lost-and-found items on campus.
  • • We compared ideas using an impact vs. effort matrix to stay realistic.

Shortlisted Ideas

Our top three were:

  1. Pet Hotel Booking System – for busy owners needing short-term pet care.
  2. Vehicle Rental Management – tracking bookings and availability for student vehicles.
  3. Community Waste & Cleanup Platform – helping residents manage bulky waste and create small paid clean-up tasks.

After some discussion, we realized the third idea, Ecolink, aligned best with HCI principles — a real social impact problem where human behavior and usability play a big role.

Choosing “Ecolink”

We spent another session sketching how Ecolink could work. We imagined residents posting waste pickup requests, others volunteering or offering cleanup help, and councils monitoring disposal patterns. We planned to integrate simple features like notifications, local maps, and a friendly UI — all grounded in HCI principles of accessibility and feedback.

Proposal Writing

Once we agreed on Ecolink, we divided roles: Iman drafted the problem statement, Zaidi refined the scope and objectives, Fuad handled the concept visuals, Izzat prepared the timeline, and Shahidah reviewed the final wording. We also started referencing sample HCI proposals to match the required structure — introduction, background, objectives, scope, and expected output.

Reflections

This week taught us that a good HCI project doesn’t start with code — it starts with questions. We learned to balance creativity with feasibility and to listen when someone said, “Cool idea, but can people actually use it easily?”

“Design is a team sport — and brainstorming is our first match.”
