Problem statement

Many residents lack a single, easy channel to manage bulky waste, e-waste, and recycling. Information is scattered across multiple platforms which makes people miss collection days and struggle during urgent or out-of-schedule disposal. Booking RoRo bins and paying permits often requires separate systems which slows everything down. Unclear e-waste guidance leads to unsafe disposal. The result is confusion, illegal dumping, hygiene issues, and low participation in sustainable practices.

Proposed solution

Ecolink is a centralized platform for residents, councils, and contractors. It unifies disposal info, booking, payments, reporting, and community action. Users can locate recycling and e-waste points, schedule bulky pickups, book RoRo bins with permits, submit reports on missed collections or illegal dumping, and discover or offer micro jobs for cleanup. Councils can post announcements and manage responses in one place.

Key features

  • Recycling and e-waste locator
  • Bulk waste pickup scheduling
  • RoRo bin booking with permit and payments
  • Issue reporting to councils with photo and GPS
  • Community gig jobs for cleanup tasks
  • Council announcement hub for campaigns and schedules

SDG alignment

  • SDG 8 Decent Work and Economic Growth

Target users

  • Urban residents need clear disposal options and reminders
  • Gig workers seek verified local cleanup jobs
  • Local councils manage schedules, complaints, announcements
  • Private contractors handle RoRo logistics and bulky pickups

Submission checklist

  • Clear problem statement
  • Personas with tasks and pain points
  • Scope and out of scope
  • User stories and key flows
  • Metrics and validation plan
  • Team roles and responsibilities
