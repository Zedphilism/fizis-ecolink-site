EcoLink — Usability Testing

Phase: Evaluation

This page documents the briefing script and task cards used to run usability testing on the EcoLink prototype for three core flows: booking a RORO bin, locating disposal centres, and lodging complaints.

Briefing Test Instructions Task Cards Prototype Feedback Form Usability Result & Insight Report

Usability Testing Briefing

Thank you for participating in this usability test. Today, you will be interacting with a prototype of a system designed to help users:

  • Book a RORO bin
  • Locate waste disposal or recycling centres
  • Lodge waste-related complaints

Before we begin, please note that this is a prototype, not the final product. Some functions are not fully optimised yet. For example:

  • Attaching photos may not work as expected or may use placeholder screens.
  • Map accuracy and live location detection are limited and may not reflect real-world data.
  • Some buttons or pages may be non-functional or only partially interactive.
  • The system may not save data because it is not connected to a full backend.

These limitations are normal at this stage of development. We are mainly evaluating the interface design, clarity, task flow, and ease of use, not full functionality.

Test Instructions

During the test, you will be given three tasks to complete using the EcoLink prototype.

  1. I will read each task to you.
  2. You may read the task card in your own time.
  3. Please speak your thoughts aloud while using the system.
  4. If you feel stuck or unable to proceed, say “terminate”, and we will continue to the next task.

Remember, we are testing the system, not you. There are no right or wrong answers — your confusion or hesitation helps us improve the design.

Your screen and voice may be recorded for analysis, but your identity will remain confidential. Do you have any questions before we start?

Task Performance Overview

Participants were asked to complete three core tasks. Scores below are based on Likert scale responses and qualitative feedback.

Task 1

Book RORO Bin

4.5 / 5

Users found the booking flow smooth and easy to follow. The main confusion was around how to extend the bin duration.

Task 2

Locate Waste Disposal

4.5 / 5

Map and search features are understandable. Users strongly expect auto location to quickly detect nearby disposal centres.

Task 3

Lodge Complaint

4.5 / 5

Complaint flow is clear and straightforward. Users can select a category, describe the issue, and submit without difficulty.

What Worked Well vs What Needs Improvement

✓

Strengths

  • Interface is intuitive and aligns with common app behavior.
  • Participants were able to complete all three tasks without major blockers.
  • Users reported feeling confident using the system, even without prior training.
  • The overall flow from booking to complaint is perceived as logical and consistent.
!

Improvement Opportunities

  • Add an auto location option to make it faster to find nearby disposal or recycling centres.
  • Clarify how RORO bin extension works, including duration rules and what happens after extension.
  • Use helper text or tooltips on key actions for first time users, especially around booking and extension.
  • Consider microcopy to reassure users about data accuracy and submission status.

Interpretation

Overall, the EcoLink prototype is already performing well for core tasks. Participants with different backgrounds and levels of experience were able to complete all three tasks and rated the system highly on ease of use and confidence.

The ratings and comments show that most friction comes from clarification rather than from broken flows. Users want smarter support for location and clearer messaging for time based actions such as extending the RORO bin booking.

This suggests that the next iteration should focus on refining details and adding guidance, rather than redesigning the entire interaction.

Planned Improvements for Next Iteration

  • Add a "Use my location" button and automatic pin placement for nearby disposal and recycling centres.
  • Redesign the RORO bin extension step with clearer labels, examples, and confirmation text.
  • Introduce helper text or tooltips on key forms to support first time users during booking and complaint submission.
  • Run a second usability test round with a new set of participants to validate the updated flows and content.
