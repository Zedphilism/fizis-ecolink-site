Project 2 — User Analysis

Phase: User research & analysis

This page documents the user analysis for EcoLink. It focuses on three core personas and their key scenarios when interacting with the proposed waste management system, covering both resident and professional use cases.

Personas Scenarios

User Personas

These personas represent the key user groups for the waste management system: a residential housewife, a professional construction project manager, and an elderly gardening enthusiast. They guide design decisions for both the mobile app and web portal.

Aina Rahman

Age: 28
Occupation: Full-time housewife
Technology experience: Moderate mobile application user

Aina lives at Taman Budi, KL. Her mornings are filled with housework and caring for her children. When the communal bins near her home overflow, she worries about smells, pests, and her family’s health. She dislikes calling council offices and dealing with confusing instructions, and prefers simple mobile apps where she can solve problems in a few taps.

She expects the smart waste app to let her report issues quickly, attach photos, and see that action will be taken without needing to chase anyone on the phone.

Pains
  • Unsure which complaint category to choose.
  • Feels stressed dealing with hotlines and long menus.
  • Worries her complaints are ignored or “hilang entah ke mana”.
Gains
  • Clear confirmation that her report is received.
  • Estimated clean-up time so she can plan around it.
  • Cleaner, safer environment for her children.

Amir Fauzi

Age: 40
Occupation: Construction Project Manager
Technology experience: High, daily professional digital user

Amir oversees renovation and construction projects across multiple job sites and is responsible for waste disposal arrangements. Construction work generates large volumes of debris that must be cleared quickly to avoid safety hazards, work delays, and regulatory fines. He is comfortable with digital tools for logistics, scheduling, and finance, and values solutions that prioritise efficiency, clarity, and reliability.

Primary goals
  • Book RORO bins quickly without phone calls or paperwork.
  • Track bin availability, delivery timing, and pickup status in real time.
Secondary goals
  • Access transparent pricing and billing details.
  • Maintain proper documentation for compliance and site audits.
Pains
  • Delays caused by slow or unresponsive waste services.
  • Lack of real-time bin availability and unclear delivery timing.
  • Manual paperwork that introduces errors and wastes time.
  • Needing to call suppliers repeatedly for confirmation.
Gains
  • System that shows live availability and exact delivery slots.
  • Instant confirmations and digital receipts shareable with site teams.
  • Dependable platform that reduces miscommunication.
  • Faster workflow with less administrative hassle.

Hassan Omar

Age: 62
Occupation: Retired school teacher
Technology experience: Basic smartphone user

Pak Hassan spends most of his time at home caring for his garden and managing household maintenance. Gardening often creates waste such as plant cuttings, soil bags, broken tools, and old electrical devices. He wants to dispose of waste responsibly but is unsure where items like e-waste or chemical containers should be taken. He owns a smartphone but struggles with complicated interfaces and small text.

When using the system, his aim is to locate the nearest appropriate disposal or recycling sites easily, with readable text and simple navigation that guides him step by step.

Pains
  • Uncertain where to bring special waste (e-waste, chemicals).
  • Finds dense interfaces and small fonts difficult to read.
  • Feels overwhelmed if too many options appear at once.
Gains

Usage Scenarios

These scenarios describe how each persona uses the proposed waste management system to complete a key task: lodging a complaint, locating disposal sites, and booking a RORO bin.

Persona: Aina Rahman Resident Complaint flow

Scenario 1 – Lodge a Waste Complaint

One morning, Aina notices that rubbish bins near the children's playground in her neighbourhood have not been collected for two days. The smell is unpleasant and stray animals are starting to gather. Concerned about hygiene and her children's safety, she opens the waste management mobile app and selects the “Lodge Complaint” feature.

  1. She takes a photo of the overflowing bins using the in-app camera.
  2. The app automatically tags her location using GPS.
  3. Aina selects the relevant complaint category and adds a short description.
  4. She submits the complaint and receives an on-screen confirmation with a reference number.
  5. A push notification confirms the report has been sent to the municipal cleaning team.
  6. Later that day, she opens the app to check the complaint status and sees that it is “In progress”.
Design considerations & alt paths
  • If GPS is unavailable, Aina can pin the location manually on a map.
  • Categories are simplified with icons and short labels to reduce confusion.
  • Status updates (Received → In progress → Completed) provide visibility and feedback.
Persona: Hassan Omar Elderly gardening enthusiast Locator flow

Scenario 2 – Locate Waste Disposal Sites

After replacing old gardening tools and clearing damaged electrical equipment from his shed, Pak Hassan needs to dispose of several items responsibly. Unsure where to bring e-waste safely, he opens the waste management application and taps on “Locate Disposal Sites”.

  1. He selects “E-waste” as the waste category and enters his residential area.
  2. The system displays a list of nearby authorised recycling centres with distance indicators.
  3. He chooses the closest centre and opens the built-in map view.
  4. Large text labels and simple icons guide him through the route step by step.
  5. He drives to the centre, confident that he is disposing of his e-waste correctly.
Design considerations & alt paths
  • High-contrast colours, bigger fonts, and minimal controls support elderly users.
  • If he prefers, directions can be opened in his default maps app.
  • Filters allow switching between categories (E-waste, Garden waste, Bulky items) without retyping his address.
Persona: Amir Fauzi Construction project manager RORO booking flow

Scenario 3 – Book a RORO Bin

Amir is preparing for debris removal at an active renovation site and requires a RORO bin to clear waste efficiently. He accesses the waste management web portal and chooses the “Book RORO Bin” service.

  1. He enters the project site location and selects the required bin size based on estimated debris volume.
  2. The system instantly shows bin availability for his dates plus transparent pricing.
  3. He confirms the preferred delivery date and time window.
  4. The portal displays a booking summary with cost breakdown and key terms.
  5. Amir submits the booking and receives a digital confirmation with a reference ID.
  6. Tracking details allow him to monitor delivery status and plan site activities accordingly.
Design considerations & alt paths
  • If no bins are available for the chosen slot, the system suggests the nearest alternatives.
  • Clear, itemised pricing builds trust for professional users handling budgets and audits.
  • Downloadable PDF/receipt supports project documentation and claims.
